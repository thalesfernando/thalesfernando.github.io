 projeto d 

Just before moving to London, I'd worked in a project with my friend Gustavo Caetano for an editorial design subject from UnB. We designed a concept of a digital magazine about the political scenario in Brazil. The idea was that the brazilian population had an alternative media for sharing and accessing relevant information about politics.

BRADO was designed to be interactive in a broad sense, allowing the audience to share each piece of content, eventually contribute with their own thoughts, and visualize surveys runned in the magazine.

Also, the logotype and name were specially designed to communicate the spirit of the publication, working with the other identity elements, such as the color pallete, with vibrant colors only possible on screen.

The logotype is an attempt to shape the more abstract aspects of the magazine, which mark its personality, the tone of its voice. We wanted the voice to have a monumental presence, but at the same time be kind and flexible, echoing like a choir.
